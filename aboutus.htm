Facts About The City of Mbabane Mbabane with an estimated population of 94 874 (2010) is the capital and largest city in Swaziland. It is located on the Mbabane River and its tributary the Polinjane River in the Mdzimba Mountains. It is located in the district of Hhohho. The City grew after the nation's administrative centre moved from Bremersdorp (now called Manzini) in 1902. It derives its name from Chief, Mbabane Kunene, who lived in the area when British settlers arrived. It lies on the MR3 road. Mbabane's closest border to South Africa is Ngwenya-Oshoek and siSwatiti is the primary language, English is widespread. Location: North-West of Swaziland; District Hhohho Climate : mild climate and snow is a rare event; Temperature: 15 °C in winter & 22 °C in summer Population : 94 874 (2010) Size : 150 km² Languages: siSwati and English The Fascinating Mbabane City This city is the home of the Waterford-KaMhlaba United World College of Southern Africa, Limkokwing University of Creative Technology, University of Swaziland, Swaziland College of Technology, and Swaziland Christian Medical University.
